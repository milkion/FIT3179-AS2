Drug Addiction in Malaysia

This visualization presents trends in drug addiction in Malaysia from 2000 to 2021, highlighting changes in case numbers, drug types, and demographics. It provides an overview of the evolving drug abuse landscape and its potential connections to social and policy shifts during this period.

Drug Addicts per 100,000 Population by State

This choropleth map visualizes the number of drug addicts per 100,000 people across Malaysia. Use the slider to explore data for different years from 2000 to 2021. States with the highest and lowest values are highlighted with text annotations, allowing for easy comparison of the most and least affected regions over time.

Number of Addicts by Drug Type

This bar chart displays the number of drug addicts by drug type in Malaysia. Heroin and Morphine were the most commonly used drugs until 2012. Afterward, a shift occurred due to changes in drug trafficking routes and the rise of synthetic drugs, leading to a surge in Opiate and Methamphetamine use.

Drug Addicts by Age Group Over Time

This stacked line chart shows drug addicts by age group between 2000 to 2019. By default, it highlights the 40 and above age group, which consistently shows the highest numbers, while the below 13 age group has the lowest values. Users can select different age groups from the dropdown menu to view specific trends for each group.

Drug Addiction by Sex Over Time

This area chart illustrates drug addiction by sex between 2000 to 2019, showing that males consistently dominate the number of addicts. On average, about 1 in 6 drug users are female, highlighting a significant gender disparity in addiction rates.
